# HIV Self-Testing

Source notes: Adapted from WHO self-testing guidelines and HIV.gov. Localized for Tanzania.

## What is HIV Self-Testing?

HIV self-testing lets you test for HIV privately at home or any comfortable location. You collect your own sample (usually oral fluid or a finger-prick blood drop) and read the result yourself in 15-20 minutes.

## Why Self-Test?

- Complete privacy — test when and where you want
- No waiting rooms or appointments needed
- Results in 15-20 minutes
- Discreet packaging when ordered through Bepawaa
- Empowers you to know your status on your terms

## How to Use an HIV Self-Test Kit

1. Read the instructions carefully before starting
2. Collect the sample as directed (oral swab or finger prick)
3. Place the sample in the test device
4. Wait the specified time (usually 15-20 minutes)
5. Read your result

## Understanding Your Results

### Negative Result (Non-Reactive)
- No HIV antibodies detected
- If you had a recent exposure (within 3 months), test again after the window period
- Consider PrEP if you have ongoing risk
- Continue practicing safe sex

### Positive Result (Reactive)
- This is a preliminary result — it needs confirmation
- Stay calm. A reactive self-test is not a final diagnosis
- Visit a healthcare facility for a confirmatory test
- If confirmed, early treatment (ART) is very effective
- You are not alone — support is available

### Invalid Result
- The test did not work properly
- Use a new test kit and try again
- Follow the instructions carefully
- Contact Bepawaa support if you need help

## Important Notes

- Self-tests detect antibodies, not the virus itself
- Window period: It can take 2-12 weeks after exposure for antibodies to develop
- A negative test during the window period may not be accurate
- Self-testing supports prevention and linkage to care, not isolation
