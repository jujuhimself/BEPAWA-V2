# HIV Basics

Source notes: Adapted from WHO, CDC, and HIV.gov public health materials. Localized for Tanzania context. This is supportive information, not a diagnosis.

## What is HIV?

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that attacks the body's immune system, specifically CD4 cells (T cells). Without treatment, HIV can gradually weaken the immune system, making it harder to fight infections.

## Key Facts

- HIV is treatable. With proper medication (ART), people living with HIV can live long, healthy lives.
- HIV cannot be transmitted through casual contact: hugging, sharing food, handshakes, or mosquito bites.
- An undetectable viral load means HIV cannot be sexually transmitted (U=U: Undetectable = Untransmittable).
- Early diagnosis and treatment lead to the best health outcomes.

## How HIV is Transmitted

- Unprotected sexual contact (vaginal, anal, or oral sex)
- Sharing needles or syringes
- Mother-to-child during pregnancy, childbirth, or breastfeeding (preventable with treatment)
- Blood transfusion with unscreened blood (rare in modern healthcare)

## How HIV is NOT Transmitted

- Kissing, hugging, or touching
- Sharing food, drinks, or utensils
- Toilet seats or swimming pools
- Mosquito or insect bites
- Coughing or sneezing

## HIV Testing

- Testing is the only way to know your HIV status
- Rapid tests give results in 15-20 minutes
- HIV self-test kits are available for private, at-home testing
- A negative result means no HIV was detected (window period: test again after 3 months if recent exposure)
- A positive result should be confirmed with a follow-up test at a healthcare facility

## Treatment (ART)

- Antiretroviral therapy (ART) is taken daily
- ART reduces viral load to undetectable levels
- People on effective ART live normal lifespans
- Treatment is available and affordable in Tanzania

## Living with HIV

- Take your medication consistently every day
- Attend regular clinic visits
- Eat nutritious food and exercise
- Seek emotional support when needed
- You can have healthy relationships and children with proper medical guidance
