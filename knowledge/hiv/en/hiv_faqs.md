# HIV Frequently Asked Questions

Source notes: Compiled from WHO, CDC, HIV.gov, and common patient questions. Localized for Tanzania.

## Testing

### How often should I test for HIV?
If you are sexually active, test at least once a year. If you have higher risk factors (multiple partners, STI history, partner with HIV), test every 3-6 months.

### Can I trust a self-test result?
HIV self-tests are highly accurate when used correctly. A negative result is reliable (outside the window period). A positive (reactive) result must always be confirmed at a healthcare facility.

### What is the window period?
The window period is the time between HIV exposure and when a test can detect it — usually 2-12 weeks depending on the test type. If you test negative during this period, test again after 3 months.

### Will anyone know I tested?
Self-testing is completely private. Through Bepawaa, test kits are delivered in plain packaging. Your results are yours alone.

## Prevention

### What's the difference between PrEP and PEP?
- PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis): Daily pill taken BEFORE exposure to prevent HIV
- PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis): Emergency treatment started AFTER possible exposure (within 72 hours)

### Does PrEP protect against other STIs?
No. PrEP only prevents HIV. Use condoms for protection against other sexually transmitted infections.

### Can I get PrEP without telling my partner?
Yes. PrEP is your personal health decision. You are not required to disclose PrEP use to anyone.

## Living with HIV

### Can I live a normal life with HIV?
Absolutely. With consistent treatment (ART), people with HIV live long, healthy, and fulfilling lives. Many have families, careers, and active social lives.

### What does U=U mean?
U=U means Undetectable = Untransmittable. When HIV treatment reduces viral load to undetectable levels, HIV cannot be sexually transmitted.

### Can I have children if I'm HIV positive?
Yes. With proper medical care and treatment, people living with HIV can have healthy, HIV-negative children. Talk to your healthcare provider about planning.

### Will I face discrimination?
Stigma exists but is decreasing. Know your rights. HIV discrimination is wrong. Support is available through counselors, peer groups, and platforms like Bepawaa.

## Treatment

### Do I have to take medication forever?
Currently, ART is a lifelong treatment. Research into cures continues. Consistent treatment keeps you healthy and prevents transmission.

### What if I miss a dose?
Take it as soon as you remember. If it's almost time for the next dose, skip the missed one. Never take a double dose. Set reminders to help with adherence.

### Are there side effects?
Most people tolerate ART well. Some may experience mild nausea, headache, or fatigue initially. Serious side effects are uncommon. Report any concerns to your provider.

## Emotional Support

### I'm scared to test. Is that normal?
Completely normal. Fear of the unknown is natural. Remember: knowing your status is the first step to protecting your health, regardless of the result.

### I feel ashamed about my status. What should I do?
Shame is often rooted in stigma, not fact. HIV is a medical condition, not a character flaw. Talk to a counselor, join a support group, or chat with Bepawaa Care.

### Where can I find support?
- Bepawaa Care chatbot (web or WhatsApp)
- Local peer support groups
- Healthcare facility counselors
- Crisis line: 116 (Tanzania)
