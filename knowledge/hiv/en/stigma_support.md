# HIV Stigma and Emotional Support

Source notes: Adapted from UNAIDS, WHO, and public mental health resources. Localized for Tanzania.

## Understanding HIV Stigma

Stigma is negative attitudes, beliefs, or discrimination toward people living with or affected by HIV. Stigma can be:
- **External**: discrimination from others (family, community, workplace, healthcare)
- **Internal (self-stigma)**: shame, guilt, or self-blame you direct at yourself
- **Anticipated**: fear of how others might react if they knew

## How Stigma Affects People

- Avoiding HIV testing out of fear
- Delaying or skipping treatment
- Social isolation and loneliness
- Anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem
- Fear of disclosure
- Avoiding healthcare facilities

## You Are Not Alone

- HIV is a health condition, like diabetes or hypertension
- Having HIV does not define your worth
- Millions of people live full, healthy, happy lives with HIV
- Treatment makes HIV manageable and prevents transmission (U=U)
- You deserve respect, care, and love — always

## Coping with Stigma

### Emotional Grounding
When stigma triggers anxiety or shame:
1. Take slow, deep breaths (in for 4, hold for 4, out for 4)
2. Remind yourself: "This is a health condition. I deserve care."
3. Name 3 things you can see around you (grounding technique)
4. Reach out to someone you trust

### Building Resilience
- Connect with peer support groups (in-person or online)
- Learn about HIV — knowledge reduces fear
- Practice self-compassion: talk to yourself as you would a friend
- Set boundaries with people who are unsupportive
- Celebrate your strengths and achievements

### Disclosure (If and When You Choose)
- Disclosure is YOUR choice — you do not owe anyone this information
- Create a disclosure plan: who, when, what to say
- Start with someone you trust deeply
- Practice what you want to say
- Have support available (a counselor or trusted friend)
- Know your rights — discrimination based on HIV status is wrong

## When Fear Prevents You from Getting Care

- You can access care confidentially
- Use Bepawaa's private chat for guidance
- Order medications or test kits with discreet delivery
- Healthcare providers are bound by confidentiality
- Your health comes first

## Seeking Professional Support

Consider talking to a counselor if:
- Stigma prevents you from taking medication or attending appointments
- You experience persistent sadness, anxiety, or hopelessness
- You feel isolated or withdrawn
- You have thoughts of self-harm

## Crisis Support

If you feel unsafe or in crisis:
- Tanzania: Call 116 or visit the nearest hospital
- Bepawaa Care: Use the confidential chat anytime
