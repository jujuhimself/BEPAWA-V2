# Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)

Source notes: Adapted from WHO and CDC PEP guidelines. Localized for Tanzania. This is supportive information — always consult a healthcare provider.

## What is PEP?

PEP is emergency HIV prevention medication taken AFTER possible exposure to HIV. It must be started as soon as possible — ideally within hours, and no later than 72 hours (3 days) after exposure.

## When to Consider PEP

PEP may be needed after:
- Unprotected sex with someone who may have HIV
- Condom breakage during sex with an HIV-positive partner
- Sexual assault
- Sharing needles or syringes
- Occupational exposure (needlestick injury in healthcare)

## The 72-Hour Window

- PEP is most effective when started within 2 hours of exposure
- It can be started up to 72 hours (3 days) after exposure
- After 72 hours, PEP is unlikely to be effective
- Every hour counts — seek help immediately

## How PEP Works

- PEP is a 28-day course of antiretroviral medications
- You take pills once or twice daily for the full 28 days
- Do not stop early, even if you feel fine
- Common side effects: nausea, fatigue, headache (usually mild and temporary)

## Getting PEP

- Go to the nearest hospital, health center, or CTC (Care and Treatment Center)
- PEP is available free of charge at government health facilities in Tanzania
- Through Bepawaa: Book an urgent PEP consultation or find the nearest provider
- Some pharmacies stock PEP — check availability on Bepawaa

## After Starting PEP

- Complete the full 28-day course
- Get tested for HIV at baseline, 4-6 weeks, and 3 months after exposure
- Use condoms during and after PEP treatment
- Report any severe side effects to your healthcare provider
- Consider PrEP for ongoing prevention if you have recurring risk

## Important Notes

- PEP is emergency prevention, not a regular prevention method
- If you frequently need PEP, ask about PrEP (pre-exposure prophylaxis)
- PEP does not protect against other STIs — get tested for those too
- You deserve care without judgment. Healthcare providers are there to help.
